Project Name:

Nutrient Checker


OVERVIEW

The most basic components of soil are minerals, organic matter, water and air. Soil minerals and organic matter hold and store nutrients, soil water and moves nutrients to plants and air plays an integral role, since many of the microorganisms need air to undergo the biological processes that release nutrients into the soil. Soil organic matter (SOM) not only stores nutrients in the soil but also is a direct source of nutrients. Its presence is considered an important indicator of fertile soil.

Minerals and organic matter are generally stable although, if not managed properly, may soon be depleted from the soil. The water and air components, however, are more dynamic and constantly change as the soil becomes wet or dry.

Importance of some nutrients are:

  • Water
    • It is essential for the life of plants and organisms in the soil.
    • It provides a pool of dissolved nutrients that are readily available for plant uptake.
    • It is necessary to weather soil.

  • SOM (Soil Organic Matter)
    • It acts as a binding agent for mineral particles. This results in easily crumbled surface soils.
    • It increased the amount of water that soils can hold.
    • It provides food for soil organisms.

Nutrient balance sheets in most soils of India have been deficient and continue to be so. This is primarily because nutrient removals by crops far exceed the nutrient additions through manures and fertilisers. For the past 50 years the gap between removals and additions has been estimated at 8 to 10 Million meteric tonnes of N + P2O5 + K2O per year.


While chemical fertilizers have their place increasing plant nutrients in adverse weather conditions or during times when plants need additional nutrients, there are also several harmful effects of chemical fertilizers. Some of the harm chemical fertilizers may cause include waterway pollution, chemical burn to crops, increased air pollution, acidification of the soil and mineral depletion of the soil.

So we can imply that if we add it in less or more proportion, in both ways its going to affect the environment, and then the soil.

The project will focus on providing with all the information that one needs to know for growing crops, i.e. nturient content, water content and ph value of soil, so that one can easily add fertilizers, and water accordingly.
